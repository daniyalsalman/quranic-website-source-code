Parts of Quran

1. Al-Fatiha to Al-Baqarah (Parah 1)

Details about Parah 1: Al-Fatiha to Al-Baqarah.

This parah contains Surah Al-Fatiha, the opening chapter of the Quran, and the beginning of Surah Al-Baqarah. It includes fundamental guidelines for Muslims and is recited in daily prayers.

2. Sayaqool (Parah 2)

Details about Parah 2: Sayaqool.

This parah continues the story of the Children of Israel and addresses the need for faith, patience, and following Allah's commandments. It emphasizes the importance of belief in the unseen and trust in Allah's will.

3. Tilkal Rusul (Parah 3)

Details about Parah 3: Tilkal Rusul.

This parah focuses on the stories of previous nations, their rejection of the divine message, and the consequences they faced. It highlights the constant message brought by all prophets: the worship of the One God.

4. Lan Tanalu (Parah 4)

Details about Parah 4: Lan Tanalu.

This parah speaks about charity, the importance of sincerity, and the consequences of false intentions in worship. It provides guidelines for self-purification and repentance.

5. Wal Mohsanat (Parah 5)

Details about Parah 5: Wal Mohsanat.

This parah discusses the rules regarding marriage, divorce, and the rights of women. It emphasizes that Islam promotes justice, equity, and respect for all people, particularly women.

6. La Yuhibbullah (Parah 6)

Details about Parah 6: La Yuhibbullah.

This parah emphasizes Allah’s love for those who act justly, repent sincerely, and avoid sin. It also discusses the value of guidance and the importance of obeying Allah's commands in every aspect of life.

7. La Talu (Parah 7)

Details about Parah 7: La Talu.

This parah highlights the need for patience and steadfastness when faced with adversity. It also discusses the themes of punishment for wrongdoing and the rewards of righteousness in the sight of Allah.

8. Ya Ayyuha (Parah 8)

Details about Parah 8: Ya Ayyuha.

This parah addresses the theme of social justice, including the proper treatment of others, and the significance of equality in society. It reinforces the idea of Allah’s absolute sovereignty and the importance of humility.

9. Inna Ma (Parah 9)

Details about Parah 9: Inna Ma.

This parah underscores the concepts of sincerity in faith and action, the need for community cooperation, and following the divine path for success in both this life and the afterlife.

10. Wa A'lamu (Parah 10)

Details about Parah 10: Wa A'lamu.

This parah addresses the importance of faith in unseen matters, providing comfort for the believers, and the reminder that Allah is always aware of everything happening in the world.

11. Ya Ayyuha (Parah 11)

Details about Parah 11: Ya Ayyuha.

This parah discusses the struggle of the believers and their efforts to preserve faith amidst adversities. It teaches perseverance and reliance on Allah.

12. Al-A'raf (Parah 12)

Details about Parah 12: Al-A'raf.

This parah discusses the story of the people of 'Ad and the consequences they faced due to their disobedience. It also contains lessons about trusting Allah and following the righteous path.

13. Al-Anfal (Parah 13)

Details about Parah 13: Al-Anfal.

This parah speaks about the Battle of Badr and discusses the importance of unity and strength in the face of opposition. It also teaches that victory comes from Allah’s support.

14. Wa-idh (Parah 14)

Details about Parah 14: Wa-idh.

This parah discusses the role of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) as a mercy for all of mankind and emphasizes the importance of following the divine guidance provided in the Quran and the Hadith.

15. Fa-idha (Parah 15)

Details about Parah 15: Fa-idha.

This parah discusses the importance of repentance and seeking Allah’s forgiveness. It calls the believers to engage in righteous deeds and avoid arrogance and pride in front of others.

16. Lillahi (Parah 16)

Details about Parah 16: Lillahi.

This parah highlights the importance of charity and helping those in need, including the poor and the orphans. It reminds us of the rewards of assisting others in the name of Allah.

17. Qad Aflaha (Parah 17)

Details about Parah 17: Qad Aflaha.

This parah describes the qualities of the believers, those who have succeeded by following Allah’s commands and acting righteously. It speaks about moral and spiritual purification.

18. A'lamu (Parah 18)

Details about Parah 18: A'lamu.

This parah reminds the believers of Allah's mercy and patience, as well as the importance of sincerity in worship and obedience. It highlights the guidance provided by Allah through the Quran.

19. Wa Yudhi (Parah 19)

Details about Parah 19: Wa Yudhi.

This parah speaks about the value of good deeds and the consequences of harmful actions. It emphasizes the need for personal accountability and the importance of faith in overcoming challenges.

20. Qul Ya Ayyuha (Parah 20)

Details about Parah 20: Qul Ya Ayyuha.

This parah calls the believers to remain steadfast and to trust in the wisdom of Allah. It provides guidance on how to handle difficulties and uncertainties in life while maintaining faith.

21. Fawwala (Parah 21)

Details about Parah 21: Fawwala.

This parah discusses the importance of sincerity in actions and faith. It teaches the lessons of the past prophets and how their teachings should be applied to the current generation.

22. Idh (Parah 22)

Details about Parah 22: Idh.

This parah teaches the importance of fulfilling promises, maintaining integrity, and being conscious of one’s duties and responsibilities towards others.

23. Wa A'lamu (Parah 23)

Details about Parah 23: Wa A'lamu.

This parah emphasizes the mercy of Allah, the importance of justice, and the need to consistently seek forgiveness for one’s shortcomings.

24. La Yuhibb (Parah 24)

Details about Parah 24: La Yuhibb.

This parah discusses the importance of righteousness and avoiding the deeds that lead to harm or evil. It calls for the avoidance of arrogance and urges humility before Allah.

25. Qaddafi (Parah 25)

Details about Parah 25: Qaddafi.

This parah discusses the concept of gratitude towards Allah and the importance of recognizing His blessings. It also encourages believers to follow the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).

26. Ana (Parah 26)

Details about Parah 26: Ana.

This parah reflects on the signs of Allah and how the creation of the heavens and the earth points to His greatness. It encourages deep reflection and connection with the divine.

27. Ya Bani (Parah 27)

Details about Parah 27: Ya Bani.

This parah calls the people to remember the blessings of Allah, to be grateful, and to reflect on the nature of the universe and His ultimate power.

28. Wa-idaa (Parah 28)

Details about Parah 28: Wa-idaa.

This parah emphasizes the strength and wisdom of Allah, as well as the importance of faith and following the path that leads to salvation.

29. Qaddafi (Parah 29)

Details about Parah 29: Qaddafi.

This parah addresses the trials of the believers, urging them to persevere and trust in Allah’s plan. It reminds them of the ultimate victory for those who believe.

30. Qul Ya Ayyuha (Parah 30)

Details about Parah 30: Qul Ya Ayyuha.

This final parah calls the believers to unity, humility, and prayer. It emphasizes the importance of seeking forgiveness and relying on Allah’s mercy as the ultimate source of strength and salvation.
